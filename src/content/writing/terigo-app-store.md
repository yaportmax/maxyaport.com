Terigo is live on the App Store. It is my first app there, which feels pretty cool.

The app started as a route-library tool for myself. I wanted a better way to pull in saved Strava routes, organize them, browse them on a map, prep routes for offline use, and follow them from one place instead of bouncing between a bunch of different apps and tabs.

Getting it through App Review was more work than I expected, but it was also a good forcing function. There is a different kind of clarity that comes from having to package something up, write the privacy and support pages, make screenshots, handle review notes, and get the whole thing into a real storefront.

I am sure I will keep changing it, but version 1.0 is out. That is enough for now.
