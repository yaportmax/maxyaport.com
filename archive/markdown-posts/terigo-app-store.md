Terigo is live on the App Store!

The app started as a route-library tool for myself. I wanted a better way to pull in saved Strava routes, organize them, see them on a map, and stop losing track of the routes I had already saved.

Right now it can import saved Strava routes, search them, filter by sport, start area, distance, climb, surface, and tags, share routes, download offline bundles and maps, and open a full-screen activity tracker that follows the saved route with progress and remaining distance. I have been testing it on very long runs, which is where the whole thing starts to matter. App Store review was trickier than I expected, but it was an interesting learning process. The app is here: [Terigo on the App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/terigo/id6761634158).
